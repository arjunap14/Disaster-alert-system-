Disaster Safety Information

Essential guidelines to keep you and your family safe during emergencies

General Safety Guidelines

Before Any Disaster

  • Create an emergency plan with family meeting spots and evacuation routes
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, medications, and supplies for at least 3 days
  • Learn how to turn off utilities (gas, water, electricity) in your home
  • Keep important documents in a waterproof, portable container
  • Stay informed about potential risks in your area

During Any Disaster

  • Stay calm and follow your emergency plan
  • Listen to official alerts and instructions from authorities
  • Help children, elderly, and people with disabilities first
  • Avoid using phones except for serious emergencies to keep lines clear
  • If evacuating, take your emergency kit and follow designated routes

After Any Disaster

  • Check yourself and others for injuries
  • Be aware of new safety issues created by the disaster (downed power lines, gas leaks, etc.)
  • Listen to news reports for information about relief efforts
  • Use text messages or social media to communicate to keep phone lines open
  • Document any property damage with photographs for insurance claims

Specific Disaster Preparedness

Earthquakes

  • Before: Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls
  • During: Drop, Cover, and Hold On under sturdy furniture
  • During: Stay indoors until shaking stops and it's safe to exit
  • After: Check for gas leaks before using matches or electrical switches
  • After: Be prepared for aftershocks

Floods

  • Before: Know your area's flood risk and elevation
  • During: Move to higher ground immediately if instructed
  • During: Never walk, swim, or drive through flood waters
  • After: Avoid flood water as it may be contaminated
  • After: Be aware of areas where flood waters have receded

Cyclones

  • Before: Install storm shutters or board up windows
  • During: Stay in an interior room away from windows
  • During: Beware of the eye of the storm - conditions may temporarily improve
  • After: Avoid flooded roads and washed out bridges
  • After: Use flashlights instead of candles to prevent fires

Emergency Kit Checklist

  • Water: 1 gallon per person per day (3-day supply)
  • Food: 3-day supply of non-perishable food
  • First aid kit: With essential medications
  • Flashlight: With extra batteries
  • Whistle: To signal for help
  • Dust masks: To help filter contaminated air
  • Tools: Wrench or pliers to turn off utilities
  • Cell phone: With chargers and backup battery
  • Cash: ATMs may not work during power outages
  • Important documents: Copies of insurance, IDs, bank records
  • Blankets: Or sleeping bags for warmth
  • Personal hygiene items: Sanitation and personal care
